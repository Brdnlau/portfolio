Computer Science & Software Engineering (2017-2018)

MIT App Inventor

September 2017 - October 2017

This project was definitely one to commemorate. It introduced us to the process of app development and gave us creative freedom over our apps. The platform we used to develop our app was on MIT App Inventor. App inventor had an environment similar to Scratch, but had unique features such as its own coding language, a live preview and the ability to utilize mobile deviceâ€™s functions. We were asked to build on from existing tutorials, and to keep a project log to document the progress we made. This project log consisted of our brainstormed ideas, a flow chart, and a time log. My partner, Apoorva Subramanian, and I worked diligently, dividing the work evenly and fairly. In the duration of this project, we added new aspects to the game such as: limited amount of bullets, a blue and purple balloon, and a leveling system. The most important concept I took away from this was the need to constantly run our code and make sure everything works the way we want it to. Brainstorming at the beginning of this project also led to our success, as coding with a clear objective in mind was much easier than making something up.
